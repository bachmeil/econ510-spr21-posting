Make a forecast of inflation one year from now using the Phillips curve if the unemployment rate is 2%. Then 6%.

Answer: Plugging into the Phillips curve, we have inf = 0.03 - 0.5(0.02) = 0.02 or 2% when the unemployment rate is 2%, and inf = 0.03 - 0.5(0.06) = 0.00 or 0% when the unemployment rate is 6%. Higher unemployment today implies lower future inflation if it's due to demand shocks.

The Federal Reserve uses the Taylor rule

i = 0.04 + 0.5(Einf - Tinf) + 0.5(Tu - u).

Answer: If the first term is bigger, that means there's higher expected inflation. You need a higher interest rate in order to reduce inflation. If the second term is negative, that means you have a high unemployment rate, so you want to reduce the interest rate.
