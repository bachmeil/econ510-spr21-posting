The Multiplier

An interesting thing about our example in Thursday's class was that:
  • Investment rose by 100
  • GDP rose by 400
This is known as the multiplier. GDP (total production) increases by more than the shock to demand. In this case, the multiplier is 4, since GDP rose by 4 times as much as the demand shock (page 54).

Multiplier Confusion

This is confusing to some students the first time they see it. What about this equation?

GDP = Demand

It seems like we're increasing the right side of the equation by 100, the left side by 400, and claiming they're still equal.

Multiplier Confusion

The source of confusion is an incorrect understanding of two things:
  • The timing of events
  • The definition of equilibrium
