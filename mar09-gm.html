Investment is given by 400 - 25i. Compute investment for i=5 and i=10. Does investment rise or fall in response to a higher interest rate? Why?



Please enter your answer

Answer: For i=5, investment is 400 - 25(5) = 275.
For i=10, investment is 400 - 25(10) = 150.

When the interest rate rises, investment falls. As we discussed in class, a higher interest rate makes some potential investment projects unprofitable, so they are not undertaken and investment falls.



What will happen to GDP as the interest rate rises from i=5 to i=10?



Please enter your answer

Answer: Investment will fall due to the higher interest rate, which causes demand to fall, as does GDP since firms produce the amount that is demanded.

What does this imply about the relationship of the interest rate and demand in the goods market model?

Answer: Since investment is a component of demand, demand falls when investment falls.
