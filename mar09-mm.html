Total savings in the economy (in billions) is given by 250 + 10i. If businesses want to borrow $350 billion to fund their investment, what does the interest rate have to be?



Please enter your answer

Answer: Borrowing $350 billion requires savings of $350 billion. Solving

350 = 250 + 10i

for i, we get an interest rate of i=10. Note that i is the interest rate.



Redo the above, but assuming businesses have decided to increase borrowing to $400 billion. What happens to the interest rate in general if businesses increase their demand for loans?



Please enter your answer

Answer: Redoing the calculation with the new required savings amount:

400 = 250 + 10i

and solving for i, we get i=15. This is consistent with the general case where it's necessary to raise the interest rate in order to get people to save more.



Assume the demand for business loans is 500 - 20i. What is the demand for business loans when i=10? When i=5?

Answer: Plugging in those interest rates, we get

500 - 20(10) = 300

Is the change in demand for business loans as the interest rate falls consistent with our discussion from class?



Please enter your answer

Answer: Yes. A higher interest rate means there are fewer profitable projects to undertake, which in turn drives down the demand for loans. This is what the above calculations showed.



In the above problems, we've seen that an increase in the desire of businesses to borrow causes the interest rate to go up, while an increase in the interest rate causes the demand for loans to go down. Explain this "paradox".



Please enter your answer

Answer: There is no paradox. Many students think there is a paradox, though, because they have difficulty keeping track of the ordering of events. In the first case there is an increase in the demand for loans that causes the interest rate to rise. In the second case there is an increase in the interest rate that causes the demand for loans to fall. The shocks and responses are different. In the first case, we're studying the effect of a change in the demand for loans. In the second case, the effect of a change in the interest rate.
