What happens to the nominal exchange rate right after the shock occurs? How does this affect US exporters and import-competing US producers?



Please enter your answer

Answer: The higher interest rate causes the demand for dollars to rise. Therefore the nominal exchange rate rises. Since prices don't change in the short run, the real exchange rate is higher. It is harder for US companies to export and import-competing companies face tougher competition.
